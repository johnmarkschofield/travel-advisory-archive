Exercise normal precautions in **Estonia**.

**Advisory Summary**

Estonia is generally a safe destination for travelers.

Review our travel guidance for Estonia to learn how to prepare for a safe trip

### **If you decide to travel to Estonia:**

  * Be aware of your surroundings when traveling to tourist locations and crowded public venues.
  * Follow the instructions of local authorities.
  * Monitor local media for breaking news. Be prepared to adjust your plans.
  * Enroll in the [Smart Traveler Enrollment Program (STEP)](/content/tsg_aem/us/en/home/international-travel/travel-advisories/smart-traveler-enrollment-program "Smart Traveler Enrollment Program \(STEP") to get important updates and alerts from the U.S. embassy or consulate. Enrolling helps the U.S. embassy or consulate contact you or your emergency contact in an emergency.
  * Review the [Country Security Report](https://www.osac.gov/Country/Estonia/Detail "OSAC Country Security Report") for Estonia.
  * Visit the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) website for the [latest Travel Health Information](https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/destinations/list "CDC Travel health Information") for Estonia.
  * Whether you're a first time or frequent traveler, use the [International Travel Checklist](/content/tsg_aem/us/en/home/international-travel/planning/checklist "International Travel Checklist").
  * We highly recommend that you buy travel insurance before you travel. Check with your [travel insurance provider](/content/tsg_aem/us/en/home/international-travel/planning/guidance/insurance "Travel Insurance") about evacuation assistance, medical insurance, and trip cancelation coverage.
