**_There were no changes to the advisory level or risk indicators. Advisory summary was updated._**

**Exercise normal precautions** in the Marshall Islands.

**Advisory summary**

The Marshall Islands are generally a safe destination for travelers.

Review our Travel Guidance for the Marshall Islands to learn how to prepare for a safe trip. 

**If you decide to travel to the Marshall Islands:**

  * Enroll in the [Smart Traveler Enrollment Program (STEP)](https://mytravel.state.gov/s/step "Smart Traveler Enrollment Program \(STEP\)") to get important updates and alerts from the U.S. embassy. Enrolling helps the U.S. embassy contact you make it easier to locate you in an emergency. 
  * Review the [Country Security Report](https://www.osac.gov/Content/Report/2834dfbe-154a-46ed-a0be-1cbd178e8f17 "Country Security Report") for the Marshall Islands.
  * Visit the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) website for the [latest Travel Health Information](https://wwwnc.cdc.gov/travel/destinations/traveler/none/marshall-islands "CDC latest Travel Health Information") for the Marshall Islands.
  * Whether you're a first time or frequent traveler, use the [International Travel Checklist](https://travel.state.gov/en/international-travel/planning/checklist.html "International Travel Checklist").
  * We highly recommend that you buy travel insurance before you travel. Check with your [travel insurance provider](https://travel.state.gov/en/international-travel/planning/guidance/insurance.html "Travel Insurance") about evacuation assistance, medical insurance, and trip cancellation coverage.
